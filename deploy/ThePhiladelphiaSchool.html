The Philadelphia School

  • In the Fall 17-18 quarter, The Philadelphia School approached the graphic design faculty at Drexel University to rebrand their school. The faculty members saw an opportunity and created a branding class, in which they only had enough time to come up with a successful logo that The Philadelphia School liked. In the Spring 17-18 term, the Drexel faculty decided to finish up the rebrand of the school and created a second class. In that class, my group members and myself came up with this for their new brand identity.

It consists of many influences from past great designers, and incorporates The Philadelphia School's amazing values. We were influenced by Massimo Vignelli for his use of color overlay, Josef Müller-Brockmann for scale change and transparencies, and Armin Hofmann for the extreme scale change in his designs. One of our group members was in charge of research and she came found the school's diversity statement. We all agreed that the opening line of "Come as you are and we’ll learn from each other." accurately describes the school, and we decided that it will be our tagline for the rebrand.
